Chapter 2 Most Important/Interesting Points

  • Hema: Risk analysis should be done as early as possible in the software lifecycle. It is the process of identifying, estimating, and evaluating risks.

  • Japneet: There are two kinds of risk analysis.

  • Kalpana: The two are software risk analysis and planning risk analysis.

  • Ramya: Risk analysis is done by experts from within various groups inside the organization.

  • Michelle: The software risk analysis can be broken down into ten steps: (1) Brainstorming (2) Making a list of features (3) Identifying the likelihood of the risks (4) Identifying the impact of the risks (5) Assigning a numerical value to the risks (6) Computing the risk priority (7) Review/modify the values (8) Prioritize the features (9) Determine the cut line (10) Consider mitigation

  • Amir: The purpose of brainstorming is to increase the ideas generated by the group.

  • Hema: The purpose of risk analysis is to determine what to test and the test priorities.

  • Ramya: Risk analysis will help to identify those components that need to be tested the most thoroughly.

  • Kalpana: Doing a formal risk analysis helps the tester during the test planning stages.

  • Danny: The point about brainstorming is to generate ideas, NOT to have a debate.

  • Mary Ann: There's no mention of how to do tie-breaking in the book for Step 8.

  • Mary Ann: There's a bug in the "Security" row of the table on page 45; that row has different content than on page 44.

  • Ramya: Increasing the user-friendliness of a product will increase the complexity.

  • Mary Ann: Read up on masked defect vs. latent defect!!!

  • Danny: A latent defect is a defect that is present but not visible.

  • Mary Ann: A masked defect is one that is hiding behind another defect.

  • Mary Ann: How does one do step 6? Multiply the number assigned to the impact by the number assigned to the likelihood.

  • Pareto Principle: 80% of the contribution comes from 20% of the contributors. In software defect terms, 20% of the modules contribute 80% of the defects.
