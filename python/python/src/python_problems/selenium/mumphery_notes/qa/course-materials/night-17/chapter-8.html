Chapter 8 Most Important Points

  1. Kalpana: The different types of test organizations are:

    • Developers
    • QA team
    • Independent test team
    • Integrated test team
    • IV&V (Independent Verification & Validation)
    • SWAT
    • Outsourcing
    • Test coordinator

  2. Ramya: Outsourcing is ideal for performance testing and web app testing.

  3. Soumya: The internal structure of, and the position of a test org depends on: skills and knowledge of employees; the corporate culture of the organization; and the politics of the organization.

  4. Hema: The independent test team is the best at providing an objective look at the SUT.

  5. Soumya: An organization can use more than one test organization structure.

  6. Kalpana: A SWAT team is a group of test experts called on in an emergency situation to do testing.

  7. Ramya: IV&V is usually performed on large high-risk projects within the DOD or other governmental agency.

  8. Soumya: The integrated test team consists of both developers and testers, who report to the same manager.

  9. Hema: A test coordinator needs to have testing skills, matrix management. He or she selects the testers late in the cycle from wherever they can be found. Hence, the volunteers rarely have proper testing experience and knowledge.

  10. Hema: The e-factor is the number of uninterrupted hours divided by the number of body-present hours.

  11. Soumya: The two advantages of developers doing the testing are:

    • No communication problems between developers and testers.
    • They have superior knowledge of the design and code.

  12. Derrick: A downside of developers doing the testing is that they don't always know the business requirements.

  13. Ramya: The test coordinator structure is used most often within large transaction-oriented companies like banks and insurance companies.
